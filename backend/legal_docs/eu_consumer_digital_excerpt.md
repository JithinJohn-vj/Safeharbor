# EU consumer & digital fairness — gambling context (excerpt)

EU consumer protection frameworks emphasize transparent contract terms, unfair terms
control, and accessible dispute resolution. Online gambling services marketed to EU
consumers must comply with national licensing and may be subject to country-specific
advertising restrictions, responsible gambling measures, and self-exclusion registers
where implemented (varies by Member State).

Cross-border services do not bypass national licensing: operators typically need a
licence in each jurisdiction where they offer services. Data protection follows GDPR:
lawful processing, transparency, and rights of access and erasure apply to player
accounts and marketing data.

This excerpt is for educational orientation only and is not legal advice.
