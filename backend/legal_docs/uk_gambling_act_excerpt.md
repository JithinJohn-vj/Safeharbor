# UK Gambling Act 2005 — lay summary (excerpt for SafeHarbor RAG)

The Gambling Act 2005 is the primary legislation governing gambling in Great Britain.
The UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) licenses operators and sets Licence Conditions and
Codes of Practice (LCCP). Social responsibility requirements include self-exclusion
schemes (GamStop for online betting), customer interaction for harm indicators, and
fair treatment of consumers.

Operators must participate in multi-operator self-exclusion where mandated. Breaches
may lead to regulatory action including fines or licence revocation. Consumers may
complain to the operator first, then escalate to Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
providers approved by the UKGC, and may involve the Gambling Commission for regulatory
matters (not individual compensation in all cases).

This text is a simplified educational summary, not legal advice.
